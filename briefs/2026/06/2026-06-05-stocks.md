# 📈 IPOs & Stock Market Watch — Friday, June 5 2026

## Headline Trends

The **Ghana Stock Exchange Composite Index** edged up 0.3% this week, driven primarily by banking sector activity. **CAL Bank** and **Ecobank Ghana** were among the volume leaders, with both counters seeing renewed institutional interest.

On the **Nigerian Exchange (NGX)**, the All-Share Index dipped 0.8% as foreign portfolio investors continued to trim positions amid ongoing **naira volatility**. The CBN's recent rate adjustments have yet to fully stabilise sentiment.

## Sentiment Snapshot

- **Ghana**: Cautiously optimistic. Local institutional investors are stepping in where foreign capital has pulled back. The financial sector is seen as a safe haven.
- **Nigeria**: Bearish short-term. The naira remains under pressure, and FX liquidity concerns are keeping foreign investors on the sidelines.
- **Jamaica**: Bullish. The JSE Main Index is up 2.1% month-to-date, with tourism and hospitality stocks leading the charge.

## Deep Dive: Ghana's Quiet Financial Sector Rally

The GSE's financial sector sub-index has outperformed the broader market for three consecutive weeks. This isn't driven by fundamentals so much as **rotation** — investors moving out of commodity-linked stocks (which had a strong Q1) into defensive banking positions.

**Key figures:**
- **CAL Bank**: +4.2% this week, P/E ratio of 3.8x — deeply undervalued by regional standards
- **Ecobank Ghana**: +2.1%, dividend yield of 8.4%
- **GCB Bank**: Flat, but trading at 0.6x book value

The opportunity here is clear: Ghanaian banks are pricing in a level of risk that doesn't reflect the actual credit environment. Non-performing loan ratios have been trending down since Q3 2025.

## Commercial Opportunity

For patient capital, **Ghanaian banking counters** offer genuine value. The dividend yields alone (7-9%) outperform most fixed-income instruments in the region, and the capital appreciation potential is meaningful if the GSE's liquidity improvements continue.

On the **Jamaica Stock Exchange**, tourism-linked stocks are worth watching as the island heads into peak travel season. **Caribbean Cement** and **GraceKennedy** have both shown strong momentum.

## Watch List

- **MTN Ghana** — Secondary listing timeline could bring significant new liquidity to the GSE
- **Nigeria pension funds** — Any increase in equity allocation rules would be a major catalyst for the NGX
- **GSE market makers** — The exchange's new market-making programme is showing early signs of improving bid-ask spreads
