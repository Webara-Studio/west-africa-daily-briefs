### 3. Africa Quietly Installed a Record 4.5 GW of Solar in 2025 — West Africa Is Leading the Charge

The Global Solar Council confirmed Africa installed 4.5 GW of solar in 2025 — nearly four times prior estimates once distributed and rooftop imports are counted. Nigeria added 803 MW and Ghana 92 MW, both ranking among the top ten African markets. Senegal switched on the region's first grid-connected solar-plus-storage facility (16 MW solar + 10 MW/20 MWh battery) at Walo Storage, with multiple 50–100 MW hybrid projects advancing. Ghana's 200 MW Norbert Anku solar park reaches first-phase completion in December 2026, with expansion plans to 1 GW by 2032.

**Why it matters:** This is a privately-financed revolution driven by diesel displacement, not policy grids — and it is fundamentally reshaping West Africa's energy economics. For commercial and industrial businesses, solar-plus-storage at grid tariffs of $0.16–0.30/kWh delivers 2–4 year payback periods without subsidies. The scale-up creates demand across the value chain: component supply, installation, maintenance, and critically, financing. The Global Solar Council identifies finance as the binding constraint; operators who solve local-currency, small-ticket lending for distributed solar will capture an enormous and still-underserved market.

### 4. Cocoa Crashes 60% — Gold Surges 23% — West Africa's Commodity Rollercoaster

Cocoa prices have collapsed from their December 2024 peak of $12,936 per tonne to approximately $3,870 — a devastating 60% decline driven by recovering African production and global inventories building to near two-year highs. The 2024/25 season recorded a modest supply surplus of 48,000 tonnes. By contrast, gold holds firmly at $4,222 per ounce, up 23% year-on-year, making it Ghana and Burkina Faso's most reliable export commodity. Nigeria's exports surged 201% year-on-year on crude oil strength, while Ghana recorded $6.2 billion in export revenue in February 2026.

**Why it matters:** The cocoa crash is an existential revenue shock for Ghana and Côte d'Ivoire, both of which depend heavily on raw bean export earnings. But it also compresses the economics in a way that favours domestic value addition — when bean margins collapse, the arbitrage shifts decisively to grinding, butter, powder, and liquor production. Africa produces 70% of global cocoa but captures less than 5% of the $129 billion chocolate industry value; this price environment should accelerate policy and private-sector investment in domestic processing capacity. Gold's strength, meanwhile, provides a fiscal cushion that governments would be wise to deploy into diversification rather than consumption.

### 5. Ghana's AgriConnect Compact Mobilises $3.5bn as COCOBOD Overhauls Its Funding Model

Ghana's AgriConnect Compact has mobilised $3.5 billion in planned investment targeting transformation across cocoa, rice, oil palm, maize, and poultry value chains — with a target of 2.6 million jobs by 2035. Simultaneously, COCOBOD announced a fundamental shift in cocoa financing for the 2026/27 season, moving away from reliance on syndicated international loans toward domestic capital markets, commercial paper, and quarterly producer price reviews. The compact brings together government, development finance, and private investors under a single coordination framework.

**Why it matters:** The $3.5bn AgriConnect Compact represents the most significant coordinated agricultural investment programme in West African history. Its cross-sector scope — from cocoa to poultry — creates opportunities not just for farmers but for input suppliers, processors, logistics firms, and financial services providers operating across these value chains. COCOBOD's funding model overhaul is equally consequential: a shift to domestic capital markets reduces foreign exchange risk for Ghana's cocoa sector and could set a template for Côte d'Ivoire. Quarterly price reviews, if implemented, would represent the most significant structural reform of Ghana's cocoa pricing in decades, improving farmer income predictability and enabling forward planning.
