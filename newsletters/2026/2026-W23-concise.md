## 1. Ghana's Crypto Sandbox Is Now Live — 11 Platforms In

Ghana's SEC admitted 11 crypto platforms (Africoin, Goldbod, KoinKoin, Vaulta, XChain and others) into its regulatory sandbox under the VASP Act 2025. This is the most concrete crypto regulatory step in West Africa. First full licences could land by September.

**Why it matters:** 79% of Africans own stablecoins — the highest rate globally. A sandbox signal from a stable democracy like Ghana tells institutional capital the door is regulated, not locked. Circle is already embedding USDC into African payment corridors, Blockchain.com expanded into Ghana after 700% Nigerian volume growth, and Western Union is launching USDPT on Solana. The infrastructure layer is being laid. Who owns the MoMo-to-stablecoin bridge?

## 2. Cocoa Crashes to 20-Month Low ($3,936/t) — Gold Surges Past $4,500/oz

Cocoa is down 61% year-on-year and approaching the cost of production for West African farmers. Meanwhile, gold hit $4,488 — its safe-haven trade fully intact. The crush spread (margin between raw beans and processed derivatives) is the widest it's been in years.

**Why it matters:** Ghana is the world's second-largest cocoa producer. At current prices, COCOBOD's GH¢2,587/bag producer price is sustainable today but vulnerable if prices fall further. The immediate play: cocoa processing and export of semi-processed products (butter, liquor, powder) while bean supply is cheap. The Damang Gold Mine's $250m equipment investment signals gold production confidence — good news for Ghana's export earnings, less so for its farmers.

## 3. Kasapreko's GSE IPO (700M Cedis) — Ghana's Biggest Consumer Listing in Years

Ghana's leading indigenous beverage manufacturer (Alomo Bitters, Storm Energy) is listing on the GSE targeting GHS 700 million. Simultaneously, Bridge Bank is preparing a $120M listing on the BRVM, and Canal+ listed on the JSE on June 3. Fitch upgraded Ghana to 'B' with a positive outlook.

**Why it matters:** The GSE Composite is up 63% YTD and 132% over 12 months — the region's strongest performer. Bank stocks trade at 3.8x P/E and 0.6x book. If this IPO prices well and trades smoothly, it opens the door for Fan Milk, Unilever Ghana, Guinness Ghana — a wave of consumer-sector listings that deepens the exchange and gives global investors what they've been waiting for: a liquid, growing African consumer story. The BRVM (up 23% YTD with CFA franc stability) is the quieter but arguably better risk-adjusted bet.

## 4. Nigeria's Kidnapping Crisis Hits the South — Telecoms FDI Collapses 91%

The mass abduction of 39 pupils and teachers in Oyo State — previously Nigeria's relatively peaceful south-west — marks a structural shift, not a blip. Sowore-led protests converged on Abuja; Aso Villa locked down. In the vacuum, federal telecoms FDI collapsed 91% as naira volatility and regulatory hostility drive investors away.

**Why it matters:** Nigeria is West Africa's largest economy. The 2027 election is 19 months away and security is already the dominant campaign issue. Businesses with Nigerian exposure should be stress-testing operations against prolonged instability. The counter-narrative: the CBN is conducting banking stress tests (winners and losers emerging), KD state is modernising governance, and AfCFTA implementation continues — the institutional architecture is being built beneath the security noise.

## 5. AethexAI Raises $3M to Do Voice AI for African Languages — At 17,000 Calls/Day

Ex-Goldman and ex-Meta founders built small models (300M–1.7B parameters) trained on local audio data that handle KYC, debt collection and customer activation in Twi, Ga, Ewe, Pidgin and French. Meanwhile in Nigeria, Tanda is digitising informal markets (Oshodi, Makola, Kejetia) and Rivia launched an asset-light healthcare SaaS model in Ghana.

**Why it matters:** This is the app thesis for the next cycle. Off-the-shelf AI fails in Africa because of latency, dialect variation and lack of local training data. The winners are building small, local, vertical. AethexAI proved the model at scale. The three open gaps: i) vernacular voice KYC for MoMo onboarding, ii) informal sector business tools with MoMo integration (10M+ potential users), iii) healthcare aggregation apps serving Ghana's fragmented clinic market. All three need USSD fallback.
